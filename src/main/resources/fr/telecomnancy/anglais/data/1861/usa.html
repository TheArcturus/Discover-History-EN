United States in 1861

The Civil War

The Civil War also called the Secession War is one of the most painful war in the story of United-States. It opposed the Union (The North) directed by Abraham Lincoln which is a group of states that were against slavery, and the Confederacy (The South) a group of secessionist states directed by Jefferson Davis.


The election of Abraham Lincoln

When Abraham Lincoln has been elected in 1860, president of theUnited States of America, because he was against slavery and wanted to abolish it, some states such as South Carolina has decided to leave the Union and create their own agreement the Carolina Ordinance of Secession. Shortly after, six other states such as Mississippi, Florida, Alabama, Texas joined them, and together they created the Confederacy.



The beginning of the war

The war began the 12 of April when the Confederacy attacked a military appliance in Fort Sumter that belonged to the Union. As a consequence, Lincoln has decided to create an army of volunteers to defend the Union and reply that offensive. It was the beginning of a succession of fight.



The consequences of the war

The War ended in 1865 with the abolition of slavery (through the Thirteenth Amendment that has been added to the United States Constitution) but also many 750 000 dead soldiers.



Quote

"Fourscore and seven years ago our fathers brought forth, on this continent, a new nation, conceived in liberty, and dedicated to the proposition that all men are created equal" Abraham Lincoln.

Fact

The Civil War was historically the most murderous war in United States.
