South Africa in 1994

Freedom Day honours

This day represents the symbol of freedom and the end of oppression for all Black South Africans.


Apartheid in South Africa

Apartheid witch is the separation of people of a society according to their races, was officially implemented in South African in 1948 and up to 1994. It caused the stigmatisation of Black south africans and discrimination in the country according to citizen's skin colour. White people were privileged while Black indigenous were not even allowed to vote or participate in the political life. The few who wanted to express political opinions were put in the jail of Robben Island.


The first non racial-election in south Africa

In 27th of April, the first election where all races were allowed to participate and vote took place for the first time. The 10th of May, the African National Congress headed by Nelson Mandela won the election with a majority, and Mandela became the first Black president.


Quote

"Never, never and never again shall it be that this beautiful land will again experience the oppression of one by another" Nelson Mandela

Fact

19 parties took part in this first non-racial election
